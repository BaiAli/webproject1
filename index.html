Types of sheep

We have the finest 4 types of sheep

Merino

Merino is a breed of fine-wooled sheep, the largest livestock of which is in Australia. Merino sheep differ from other sheep breeds in high quality worsted (combed) wool, consisting of thin (15-25 microns) soft fibers.

Texel

The Texel is a breed of domestic sheep originally from the island of Texel in the Netherlands. A heavily muscled sheep, it produces a lean meat carcass and will pass on this quality to crossbred progeny.

Dorper

Dorper is a new hairless breed of sheep for meat production, bred in 1966 by crossing Dorset and Persian types of sheep by the South African Department of Agriculture.

Rambouillet

The Rambouillet is a breed of sheep classified of the genus Ovis. It is also known as the Rambouillet Merino or the French Merino.
